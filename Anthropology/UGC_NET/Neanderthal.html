Introduction to Neanderthals

Neanderthals (Homo neanderthalensis) were a species or subspecies of archaic humans that lived in Europe and parts of Asia.

1. Discovery of Neanderthals

The first fossils attributed to Neanderthals were discovered in 1856 in the Neander Valley of Germany.

2. Physical Characteristics

  • Short, stocky build adapted for cold climates
  • Large brow ridge and heavyset face
  • Robust bones and strong muscles

3. Tools and Culture

Neanderthals used tools and exhibited some cultural behaviors such as burial practices and symbolic behavior.

4. Extinction and Legacy

Neanderthals went extinct around 40,000 years ago, but modern humans carry traces of Neanderthal DNA.
